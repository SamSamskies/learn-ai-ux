Text-Surface Wrap-Up

Eight builds shipped. This lesson closes the text arc: audit what you have, write the before/after story, and leave with something you can show in a portfolio review or team demo.

Why this, why now

Your mission success criterion isn't "I added features." It's I can point to a before/after and explain the UX decisions behind it. That story is the differentiator — frontend engineers who can articulate why an AI surface feels trustworthy beat engineers who only wire up streaming.

You started with a capable Phase 1 demo: streaming, labeled steps, structured output. You ended with deliberate lifecycle states, legible waits, calibrated trust, and human-in-the-loop at three decision points. This lesson makes that delta explicit.

The arc in one breath

Lifecycle (0001–0004) → Trust (0005, 0007) → Human-in-the-loop (0006, 0008). Perceived latency runs through all of it — acknowledgement, named steps, strategic friction where risk is real.

Primary source (skim) Shape of AI — scan the six categories and note which patterns you shipped. Use as vocabulary when you tell the story. See also the retrofit checklist.

Build it (~20 min)

  1. Run the audit. Open the retrofit checklist beside your running app. Walk each row — lifecycle, trust, human-in-the-loop, cross-cutting. Mark anything that fails. Most learners pass 10+ of 12; one or two gaps is normal.

    Common last-mile misses: scroll-on-send, thinking indicator conflated with streaming cursor, refine banner not clearing on manual edit.

  2. Fix one gap (if any). Pick the highest-impact fail and patch it now — not a refactor, one focused diff. If everything passes, skip to step 3.

  3. Add docs/UX-RETROFIT.md to your repo. This is the tangible artifact. Paste and fill in:

    # UX Retrofit — Research Assistant (Phase 2)

## Before (Phase 1 demo)
- Streaming worked; lifecycle states were accidental
- Errors surfaced raw SDK text
- Trust was implicit — sources existed but weren't scannable
- No confirmation on session reset; no prompt assistance

## After (Phase 2 product surface)
| Area | Decision | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| Lifecycle | derivePhase + distinct submitted/thinking/working | Users need to know *where* they are in a nondeterministic wait |
| Stopped | preserve partial + regenerate in place | Abort isn't failure — it's a user choice |
| Errors | friendly transport mapping | Raw errors erode trust and aren't actionable |
| Trust | TrustBar + inline citations via post-processing | Scan-height provenance + claim-level verify; don't bet UX on model discipline |
| HITL | confirm new chat; edit-before-send Refine | Strategic friction matches harm × scope × reversibility |

## Demo script (~90 sec)
1. Send a vague question → Refine → edit one word → Send
2. Point at TrustBar while brief streams
3. Click an inline citation marker → source list
4. Stop mid-stream → show partial preserved → Regenerate
5. New chat with history → confirm dialog names what gets lost

## One-liner for interviews
"I retrofitted a streaming research demo into a product-grade text surface —
 deliberate lifecycle states, calibrated trust signals, and human-in-the-loop
 at destructive, verification, and pre-run decision points."

  4. Record evidence. Capture one of:

    • Screenshot pair — before (if you have one) + after of the same flow, saved in docs/
    • 30–60 sec screen recording — follow the demo script above
    • PR description — if you shipped Phase 2 as a branch, paste the table from UX-RETROFIT.md into the PR body

    You only need one. The goal is a link you can drop in Slack or a portfolio.

  5. Self-grade with retrieval. Cover the checklist, then recall from memory — no peeking:

    • Name all seven lifecycle-related states your app handles
    • Explain why status === 'streaming' alone isn't enough for the thinking gap
    • Give one example each of strategic friction vs instant action in your app

Self-check

Why write UX-RETROFIT.md instead of just remembering?

Retrieval practice builds storage strength. Writing the decision log forces you to articulate *why*, not just *what* — which is exactly what portfolio reviews and design critiques ask for.

What if I find three checklist failures?

Fix the highest-impact one today; log the others as backlog in UX-RETROFIT.md. Perfect isn't the bar — explainable, deliberate UX is.

Is tool-approval HITL missing?

Yes — intentionally out of scope for this text arc. Agents and tool approval are a later roadmap phase. You covered the three HITL surfaces that matter most on a research chat: destructive confirm, claim verify, pre-run intent.
