Voice Arc Wrap-Up

Eleven builds later: you know how Realtime UX is supposed to work — and you've decided OpenAI's realtime voice models aren't worth shipping. This lesson closes the arc: audit the patterns, lock the verdict, move on.

Why this, why now

Mission success isn't "I tried every API." It's I can design interfaces over nondeterministic AI — and I know when the stack fails the product bar. Text retrofit raised the research assistant. Voice taught the harder constraints (turn-taking, latency, STT loss, multimodal companions). The honest outcome: polish cannot rescue unreliable speech-to-speech.

Image output sealed it — gated prompts, cheap models, still results you wouldn't use. That's product judgment, not a failed lesson.

The arc in one breath

Lifecycle & turns (0010–0013, 0016) → Product shell (0014–0015) → Multimodal & tools (0017–0020). Through-line: voice for the headline, screen for precision — and escape to Research when STT or quality fails.

Product verdict

OpenAI Realtime voice is not a product surface for your apps. Keep /voice as a lab if you want; don't invest more cycles chasing speech-to-speech quality on this stack. Research text remains the thing you'd actually use.

Primary source (skim) Voice arc checklist — audit what shipped, then the "what transfers" list. Optional skim: AI Design Patterns · Voice ("when voice needs a visual companion") — you already lived that constraint.

Do this (~15 min)

  1. Run the checklist once. Open the voice arc checklist beside the app. Mark what you actually shipped. You're not chasing a perfect score — you're confirming the lab is complete.

  2. Name one pattern you'd reuse on text (or the next surface). Pick from memory, then check the checklist's "What transfers" list. Examples that usually stick:

    • Split-by-risk tool approval (auto lookup vs gate side effects)
    • Confirm before destroying route-local state
    • Handoff that lands in an editable composer, never auto-runs
    • Don't trust STT / lossy input for irreversible or expensive actions

  3. Decide the fate of /voice (one sentence). Leave it as a probe, hide it, or delete later — your call. Write the sentence somewhere you'll see it (NOTES, a code comment, or just tell me). Default recommendation: leave the probe; don't build on it.

  4. Self-grade with retrieval. Cover the checklist, recall without peeking:

    • Why silence_duration_ms: 500 beat semantic VAD for your Q&A use case
    • Why image prompts (and research briefs) need an on-screen edit gate
    • What still completes after the user rejects a Realtime tool call

Self-check

Did the voice arc fail?

No. You learned the interaction design and proved the stack isn't product-ready for you. A wrap-up that ends in "don't ship this" is success — false optimism would be the failure.

Should I delete all the Realtime code?

Not required. A dormant probe is fine documentation of what you tried. Delete only if it clutters the product you use daily.

What if another provider's voice is better later?

Reuse the checklist patterns; don't reuse the "trust STT / auto-generate images" assumptions. Re-evaluate quality the same way you did here.
