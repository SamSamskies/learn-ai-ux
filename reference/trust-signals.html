Trust Signals

UI that helps users judge whether to believe an AI output — and how hard to verify it.

The three signals

SignalWhat it answersWhere it lives
Provenance What tools or retrieval backed this answer? (web search, uploaded docs, model-only) Near the headline — scan height, not a footer footnote
Sources Which specific materials were cited? Can I click through? Pills or inline markers; count visible even when the list is below
Calibrated uncertainty How well do the sources support this answer? Human copy + visual tone — never a raw enum like high

Calibrated confidence copy

Your schema already emits low | medium | high. The UI job is to translate that into honest, actionable language — not to pretend precision you don't have.

LevelUser-facing copy (example)Tone
high Well-supported by retrieved sources Neutral-positive — still invites spot-checking
medium Partially supported — verify key claims Caution — nudge toward sources
low Limited source coverage — treat as a draft Warning — don't bury this in footer text

Common mistakes

Raw enum in the UIConfidence: high reads like internal telemetry, not a trust signal.
Sources only at the bottomUsers who don't scroll never see citations — trust becomes unconditional.
False confidenceGreen checkmarks on every answer erode trust faster than honest caveats.
Hiding missing sourcesIf nothing was cited, say so. Empty states maintain trust; filler erodes it.
Citations without linksA pill with a title but no URL is decoration, not verification.

Scan-height layout

Put the trust bar directly under the headline badge — before the summary paragraph. The user should know how grounded this answer is before they read a word of synthesis.
