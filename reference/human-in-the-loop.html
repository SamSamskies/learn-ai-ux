Human-in-the-Loop

Patterns where the user confirms, edits, or approves before an AI action executes — especially when the action is irreversible or high-impact.

When to gate

Risk profileExamplesUI treatment
Low — skip or light ack Search, draft email, regenerate a reply No dialog; undo or regenerate in place is enough
Medium — confirm once Overwrite a field, send on user's behalf Inline diff or lightweight "Apply changes?"
High — strong confirm Delete session, purchase, share PII, bulk overwrite Modal with explicit consequence copy; destructive button styling

Match friction to harm × scope × reversibility. Routine tasks with easy undo should not nag; irreversible loss-of-work actions should always pause.

Destructive-action dialog checklist

Name what gets lost"Conversation and uploaded documents" — not "Are you sure?"
Default to safeFocus Cancel; Escape dismisses; backdrop click cancels
Skip when nothing to loseEmpty chat + no uploads → run immediately, no dialog
Disable while busyDon't open confirm during an in-flight stream
Accessiblerole="dialog", aria-modal, labelled title
